Title of the research project: Research on user interfaces for recruiting systems

Principal Investigator: Dr. Carlos Castillo - carlos.castillo@upf.edu Edifici Tànger UPF, C/Tànger, 122-140 08018 Barcelona (Spain)

Institution: Universitat Pompeu Fabra

Funding agency: EU Commission, under grant agreement 101070212

Objectives and duration of the project: This study is part of FINDHR, a 3-year research project funded by Horizon Europe. The study aims to understand how the outcomes of job applicants can be affected by ranking algorithms, user interfaces, and environmental factors (and their interactions). Results from the study will be used to generate a dataset of fabricated interactions of recruiters with a recruitment system (e.g., candidate bookmarking, shortlisting, profile visiting).

Methodology and participation:A user study is proposed to collect interactions of recruiters with a recruitment system. Participants who will play the role of recruiters will be invited to short-list a group of candidates who applied for a position. In particular, given a fabricated open position (e.g., a software developer with experience programming in Python), the participant (recruiter) will be asked to use a recruitment system and go through the list of candidates who presumably applied for the position and shortlist a group of them. Before concluding, you will be asked to fill out an exit questionnaire containing optional questions such as your gender and ethnicity, as well as a free-text field for additional comments. A debriefing will be conducted by the end of the session to explain to participants details about the experimentation.

Privacy:The data you provide will be kept protected with passwords and encrypted in UPF servers for the duration of the FINDHR project and will be accessible only by a limited number of researchers who will sign a data privacy commitment form. We will keep the collected data for the duration of the project to fine-tune the methods used to generate the synthetic data and to finalize research publications presenting our findings.

Compensation: For professional recruiters, there is no monetary compensation for participating. For crowdsourcing workers, the compensation is stated in the task description.

Risks and benefits: We understand that the project has both benefits and risks for participants. The main risk is the leak of the participants’ gender and ethnicity. The main benefit, on the other hand, is the development of new methods for trustworthy automated hiring systems.

Voluntary nature of participation: Your participation is voluntary, and you can withdraw from it at any time without justification. In this sense, you can exercise your right to delete your donated data by sending an email to the contact person.

Contact information: If you have any questions about this study, contact researcher Jorge Saldivar (jorge.saldivar@upf.edu).

If you have doubts, complaints, or questions about this study or about your rights as a research participant, you may contact UPF’s Institutional Committee for the Ethical Review of Projects (CIREP) by phone (+34 93 542 21 86) or by email (secretaria.cirep@upf.edu). CIREP is not part of the research team and will treat any information you send confidentially.
